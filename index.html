Import questions from Claude

Send Claude your notes or a PDF in your chat window and ask it to generate flashcards, multiple choice, or true/false questions. Claude will give you text (or a .txt file) in the import format below — paste it in, preview, and add what you want to the bank. No API key, no cost.

Import format — one question per block, separated by a blank line:

Q: What are the 5 principles of ethics?
A: Fidelity, Honesty, Justice, Respect, Beneficence
CATEGORY: Ethics

Q: Integrity means doing the right thing only when someone is watching.
A: False
CATEGORY: Ethics
GOLD: yes

Q and A are required. TYPE (standard/truefalse), CATEGORY, and GOLD (yes/no) are optional — a plain True/False answer is auto-detected as a true/false question even without a TYPE line.

How this site works

  • Study — run a study session as flashcards, multiple choice, or true/false. Filter by category or by week, and shuffle if you want. For flashcards, tap "Mark correct" to track what you actually know, or hit "Start audio session" to have it read questions and answers aloud with a pause in between — handy for studying while driving. For multiple choice and true/false, your score tracks automatically as you answer.
  • Question Bank — add questions by hand, or edit/delete anything already in there. Every question is tagged with whoever added it, plus which week it was added in.
  • Upload — ask Claude in your regular chat to turn your notes or a PDF into questions using the import format shown on the tab, then paste what it gives you here to preview and add it to the bank.
  • Calendar — shared calendar for test dates and study sessions, month/week/day views.

Gold vs. standard questions

Any question can be marked ⭐ gold — that means it's guaranteed to show up on the actual exam. Gold questions get a gold outline everywhere they appear (question bank list, flashcards, quiz questions) so they're easy to spot and prioritize. Everything else is a standard question — still worth knowing, just not flagged as guaranteed.

Weeks

Every question is automatically filed under the week it was added — Week 1 is July 13–19, Week 2 is July 20–26, and so on, each week starting Monday. Use the Week filter on the Study tab to study just this week's material, or everything so far.
